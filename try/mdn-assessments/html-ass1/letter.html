Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I didstudy exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicking dance
A little know but very influential dance dating back as far as 300 BC, whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage thrir livestock or be "fruitful"
Icelandic brownian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to pracetice this dance, which involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptably tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired modern styles such as Twerking
Arctic robot dance
An interesdting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discoverd a new dance style by "robotic", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold
